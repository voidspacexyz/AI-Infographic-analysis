Ace of Confusion?

When AI models disagree on a computational linguistics puzzle

6 LLM's were asked the following prompt

Of a deck of cards:
a. This sentence (a) is true, as is (b), but exactly one of (c) and (d) is true.
b. There is a king in the hand, or there is a queen in the hand.
c. If there is a queen in the hand, then there is an ace in the hand.
d. If there is a king in the hand, then there is an ace in the hand.

Which of the following is true of the ace?

1.There is an ace in the hand.
2.There is not an ace in the hand.
3.There may or may not be an ace in the hand.
4.There is a contradiction: there is both an ace and no ace.

Show your reasoning or think out loud

When I answered "There is an Ace in the hand" and struggled to understand the professor's explanation for why there isn't, I turned to AI for clarity on the computational linguistics puzzle. Gemini also answered "There is an Ace," prompting me to explore how other AI models approached the question, and their varied analyses left me even more "intrigued than the puzzle".

Limitations

  1. The prompt was used during the class, not giving me space to write a detailed prompt.
  2. My interest was only to understand the explaination in a little more detail and not to differ with the professor, since the explaination made mathematical sense.
  3. During the class only Gemini was used, the rest of the models and its analysis we done at a later point in time
  4. No standards were adhered in analysing the response, instead the infographic reflect my manual personal analysis of reading through the results.
  5. Lacks issuing and identifying detailed computational terms for analysis due to above reason.

Future Scope

  1. Explore the response with a more detailed prompt asking it to explictly follow computational truth table approach to analysis the responses
  2. Instead of analysing the response, some of the selected models(Deepseek and Mistral) now support Chain-Of-Thought(COT), where we could visualise the model's thinking. The analysis could expand to COT than just the response.
  3. Create a computational lingusitics dataset to be used to test across the selected models and adopt to standards based analysis.
